The Perfect Chocolate
Chip Cookie

Brown butter, two sugars, overnight rest — bakery results in your own kitchen.

The Recipe

Brown Butter, Two Sugars,
One Perfect Cookie.

The difference between a good cookie and an unforgettable one is brown butter — that nutty, toffee-scented liquid gold you get from cooking butter past melting point. Combine it with a blend of brown and white sugar, an extra egg yolk for richness, and a night's rest in the refrigerator, and you have a cookie with caramelised, lacy edges and a soft, fudgy pull-apart centre.

✦ ✦ ✦

Ingredients

What You'll Need

The Dough

  • Unsalted butter 225 g
  • Light brown sugar 200 g
  • Caster (white) sugar 100 g
  • Large eggs 2
  • Extra egg yolk 1
  • Pure vanilla extract 2 tsp
  • All-purpose flour 300 g
  • Baking soda 1 tsp
  • Fine sea salt 1 tsp

Chocolate & Finish

  • Dark chocolate chips (70%) 200 g
  • Milk chocolate chips 80 g
  • Flaky sea salt (Maldon) to taste

Method

Step by Step

  1. 1

    Brown the butter. Melt butter in a pale saucepan over medium heat, swirling often, until deep golden with a nutty aroma — about 5 min. Pour into a large bowl and cool for 15 min.

  2. 2

    Cream butter & sugars. Whisk both sugars into the cooled brown butter for 2 minutes until the mixture is glossy and ribbon-like.

  3. 3

    Add eggs. Beat in the eggs and extra yolk one at a time, then add vanilla. Mix until smooth and slightly airy.

  4. 4

    Fold in dry ingredients. Sift flour, baking soda and salt directly into the bowl. Fold until just combined — stop as soon as no dry flour remains.

  5. 5

    Add chocolate. Fold in both types of chips. Cover tightly and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, ideally overnight.

  6. 6

    Bake. Heat oven to 175 °C / 350 °F. Scoop 50 g balls onto lined trays, spaced 6 cm apart. Sprinkle with flaky salt. Bake 11–13 min — edges golden, centres looking underdone. Cool on the tray.

"Rest the dough overnight. One night in the refrigerator is worth more than any technique."

Baker's Notes

Secrets to the Perfect Batch

  • Brown butter is the single most impactful upgrade — the Maillard reaction creates over 600 new flavour compounds.
  • Cold dough holds its shape in the oven, giving you thick, chewy cookies rather than flat discs.
  • Pull cookies out when the centre looks glossy and underdone — carryover heat finishes the job as they cool on the tray.
  • Flaky salt on top amplifies sweetness and chocolate; never skip it.
  • Raw dough balls freeze beautifully for up to 3 months. Bake from frozen, adding 2–3 extra minutes.
  • For extra depth, swap 50 g flour for 50 g bread flour — the extra gluten gives a chewier bite.

Tiramisù

Espresso-soaked ladyfingers, mascarpone cream, and a whisper of dark cocoa. This is Italy in a dish.

The Recipe

"Pick Me Up" — Italy's
Most Beloved Dessert.

The name means pick me up in Italian, and it delivers exactly that: a layered assembly of crisp savoiardi soaked in strong espresso and a splash of Marsala, cushioned between clouds of mascarpone cream whisked with egg yolks and sugar. No oven required. Just patience — and a night in the refrigerator for the flavours to melt together into something extraordinary.

✦ ✦ ✦

Ingredients

What You'll Need

The Mascarpone Cream

  • Egg yolks, large 6
  • Caster sugar 120 g
  • Mascarpone, cold 500 g
  • Double cream, cold 300 ml
  • Pure vanilla extract 1 tsp

The Espresso Soak

  • Strong espresso or moka pot coffee 350 ml
  • Dry Marsala wine (or rum) 60 ml
  • Caster sugar (for coffee) 1 tbsp

Assembly & Finish

  • Savoiardi (ladyfinger biscuits) 36–40
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder to dust
  • Dark chocolate (70%), grated 40 g

Method

Step by Step

  1. 1

    Make the espresso soak. Brew strong coffee and stir in the Marsala and sugar. Allow to cool completely to room temperature. Never dip in hot coffee.

  2. 2

    Whisk egg yolks & sugar. In a heatproof bowl set over a pot of barely simmering water (bain-marie), whisk egg yolks and sugar constantly for 8–10 min until thick, pale, and tripled in volume. Remove and cool.

  3. 3

    Beat mascarpone. In a large bowl, whisk cold mascarpone with vanilla until just smooth. Do not over-beat — mascarpone can split.

  4. 4

    Whip the cream. In a separate bowl, whip cold double cream to medium-stiff peaks.

  5. 5

    Fold together. Gently fold the cooled egg yolk mixture into the mascarpone, then fold in the whipped cream in two additions. The cream should be airy and hold its shape.

  6. 6

    Dip & layer. One at a time, briefly dip each savoiardo in the coffee soak (1–2 seconds per side) and arrange in a single layer in a 30×20 cm dish. Spread half the mascarpone cream on top.

  7. 7

    Repeat & chill. Add a second layer of dipped savoiardi, then spread the remaining cream. Dust generously with cocoa powder and scatter over grated chocolate. Cover and refrigerate for at least 6 hours, preferably overnight.

"The secret to tiramisù is time. A night in the refrigerator is not optional — it is the recipe."

Chef's Notes

Making It Perfect

  • The bain-marie step cooks the yolks to a safe temperature while building volume — do not skip it.
  • Dip the savoiardi quickly — 1 to 2 seconds per side. Soggy ladyfingers make a wet, heavy tiramisù.
  • Always use cold mascarpone and cold cream: they whip more stably and the cream won't over-beat.
  • Overnight chilling (8–12 h) allows the layers to set firmly and the flavours to fully integrate.
  • Dust with cocoa powder only just before serving — it absorbs moisture and can look patchy if applied too far in advance.
  • For a non-alcoholic version, replace Marsala with a teaspoon of vanilla extract and a splash of strong cold brew.
  • Individual glasses make elegant single-serve portions — great for dinner parties.
